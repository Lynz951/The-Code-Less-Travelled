  4. Sidenote: While talking about something completely unrelated to coding class my mom said something that really has kept me going. I thought I would share it here.

    Me: "Wow, that must be hard."
    My mom: “Yeah, but that’s OK, I can do hard.”

    I am so glad to have been raised by someone with this mentality. Sometimes when I hear someone won’t do something, even though they want to, or know it’s the right thing to do, because it will be hard, I am surprised. I don’t love doing hard things anymore than most people, but I’m not afraid of hard. I don’t think just because something will be hard is a reason not to do it. I actually like doing hard things. I feel proud when I complete them. I feel like I have done something special. I did the hard thing and survived. What’s next?

Week 5

Q & A with Lindsay

  1. What is the number one thing that held you back this week? Why?

    2. Due to this being the first week of in-person, the main thing that held me back in class was my endurance. I was very tired most days, which affected my ability work with clarity and precision. I wish I could have done more work at home, but by the time I had a chance I was simply just too tired. I think that the longer I am in the program and the more that I practice I will be able to work faster and for a longer period of time each day. I hope so at least!

  2. What is one thing you would like to learn more about regarding JavaScript (JSON, Data Structures), Bootstrap, or Atomic Design?

    3. I woud like to learn much more about Bootstrap. I love making my projects asthetically pleasing and well organized. I think that Bootstrap is an awesome tool.

  3. How does In-Person Bootcamp compare to Remote Onboarding?

    4. In-Person Bootcamp is such a fun, immursive experience. I feel like I have learned so much this week. I also like the collaborations that I have participated in. The collaborations between my classmates have also been benefitial to me, just by eavesdropping when I can. I feel like I am working really well with the people that sit around me, which is very encouraging. I felt very alone in my desert of despair while Onboarding. I will say that Onboarding was benefitial in building up my mental endurance for in-person.

  4. What is your "Why"?

    5. My "why" for taking this bootcamp, and working so hard, is myself. I have spent the last 12 years mainly focused on my children. They are finally at an age when they don't need me as much. I am so ready for my own identity to come back, even if it is completely different than before. I want to be useful for something other than parenting.
